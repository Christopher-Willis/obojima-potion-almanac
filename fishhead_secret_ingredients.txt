Found among Loninni's papers — Mount Arbora relay

Dear Uncle,

Most mornings here begin with fog rolling over the peaks and the smell of woodsmoke from the cookfire.
Our watchpost is small, but the view of the valley more than makes up for the drafts in the walls.
Uncle, I have finally learned to make that fish stew you used to cook when the catch was good.
No one warned me that winters in the mountains would turn my ink to slush before breakfast.
The goats wander onto the roof sometimes, and the herders pretend not to notice.
I have counted seven full moons from the window of my post, and each one seems to roll backward across the ridge before dawn.
A stray cat has taken up residence in the supply shed and refuses to leave, no matter how often I shoo it.
Remember to tell Mara that her blue scarf is still here; I keep forgetting to pack it.
By the time this letter reaches you, the spring rains should have started down in the shallows.
On clear nights I can see the lights from the Fish Head campfires twinkling near the Gale Fields.
Rumor says a trader is coming through next week with fresh tea, and I intend to barter hard.
All my love to you and the twins — give them each a honey candy from me.
I will try to write again once the pass opens.
Until then, keep the kettle warm.

— Loninni
